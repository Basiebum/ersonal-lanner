Hyster-Yale Test Bench
2024 — FEM · Structural Design · SolidWorks Simulation — Bachelor Thesis
Overview

The Hyster-Yale Test Bench project was conducted as part of my bachelor thesis in mechanical engineering. The objective was to design and structurally validate critical components of a hydraulic test bench used for validating heavy-duty drivetrain components under realistic load conditions.

The system had to withstand high cyclic loading, significant torsional moments, and dynamic force inputs while maintaining structural stiffness and alignment tolerances required for accurate measurement results.

Engineering Challenge

The primary engineering challenge was balancing stiffness and manufacturability under strict spatial constraints. The frame components had to resist deformation under peak hydraulic loads while remaining serviceable and modular.

Key considerations included:

Max Load
High dynamic hydraulic loading (cyclic)
Material
Structural steel (S355 equivalent)
Validation
Finite Element Analysis (static + safety factor verification)
Software
SolidWorks + Simulation package
Design & Simulation Process

The design process began with load case identification and boundary condition modelling. Reaction forces from the hydraulic system were translated into simulation constraints.

A parametric CAD model was developed to allow rapid iteration of:

  • Plate thickness optimisation
  • Weld geometry refinement
  • Stiffener placement
  • Bolt connection load distribution

Multiple FEM iterations were conducted to minimise mass while maintaining acceptable stress levels below yield with an appropriate safety factor.

Results

The final design achieved:

  • Reduced peak von Mises stress compared to initial concept
  • Improved stiffness in critical mounting regions
  • Optimised material usage without compromising safety factor
  • Manufacturable weld configurations aligned with workshop capabilities

The validated model provided confidence for manufacturing and integration within the complete hydraulic testing system.
